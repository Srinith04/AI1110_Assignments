\textbf{EXAMPLE 9 :}

Three cards are drawn successively, without replacement from a pack of 52 well shuffled cards. What is the probability that first two cards are kings and the third card drawn is an ace ?

\textbf{SOLUTION :}

In a deck of 52 cards there are 4 King cards and 4 Ace cards in total. Let us take three random variables $X$,$Y$,$Z$ for three trials of drawing a card each of which takes values from the set of real numbers {0,1,2} . Let us define those events in Table \ref{Table1}, Table \ref{Table2}, Table \ref{Table3}.

We have to find $\pr{(X=0)(Y=0)(Z=1)}$.

We know that ,
\begin{align}
    \pr{(X=0)} &= \dfrac{\text{Number of Kings}}{\text{Total number of cards}} \\[10pt]
    \pr{(X=0)} &= \dfrac{4}{52}
\end{align}
and , also \pr{(Y=0)|(X=0)} is the probability of second king with the condition that one king has already been drawn . As now ,there are 3 Kings in 51 cards .
\begin{align}
    \pr{(Y=0)|(X=0)} = \dfrac{3}{51}
\end{align}
Lastly , \pr{(Z=1)|(Y=0)(X=0)} is the probability of third drawn card to be an ace ,with the condition that two kings have already been drawn . As now , there are 4 aces in 50 cards .
\begin{align}
    \pr{(Z=1)|(Y=0)(X=0)} = \dfrac{4}{50}
\end{align}
By multiplication law of probability ,we have
\begin{multline}
    \pr{(X=0)(Y=0)(Z=1)} = \\
    \pr{(X=0)} \pr{(Y=0)|(X=0)}\\
            \pr{(Z=1)|(X=0)(Y=0)}
\end{multline}
\begin{align}
    \pr{(X=0)(Y=0)(Z=1)} &= \dfrac{4}{52}\dfrac{3}{51}\dfrac{4}{50} \\[10pt]
                         &= \dfrac{2}{5525}
\end{align}
